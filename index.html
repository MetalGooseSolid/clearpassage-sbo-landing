If this is your life

You’ve been told surgery is the only option. There is another way.

Small-bowel obstruction is the second most common reason for emergency surgery in the U.S. — and for most people, it doesn’t happen only once. Adhesions form, tighten, and pull the intestine closed again. Another ER visit. Another NG tube. The quiet fear of the next episode shaping every meal and every plan.

Clear Passage® exists for the people caught in that cycle: a way to address the scar tissue itself, without a scalpel.

  • Cramping abdominal pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Bloating and swelling
  • Constipation or inability to pass gas
  • A visibly distended abdomen
  • Thin, stringy, or pencil-like stool
The therapy

A treatment measured in hands, not incisions.

Over decades, physical therapist Belinda Wurn and massage therapist Larry Wurn developed a precise, whole-body manual technique that works to deform and release adhesions — the internal scar tissue that can bind the bowel with the strength of surgical-grade glue. No drugs. No anesthesia. To most patients, it simply feels like a deep, purposeful massage.

Does it hurt?

For most people, no. Patients typically describe the work as a deep, focused massage. Your therapist adjusts pressure to your comfort throughout, and there’s no anesthesia, no incisions, and no recovery period to endure afterward.

Is this instead of surgery?

Clear Passage® is a non-surgical option that addresses the adhesions that cause obstruction. Many patients come to us specifically hoping to avoid another operation. We’ll always be honest during your consult about whether the therapy is appropriate for your situation.

How long does treatment take?

A standard program is 20 hours of hands-on therapy, delivered in two-hour sessions — often about four hours a day over a single week. You leave with a personalized plan to help maintain your results at home.

Will it work for me?

That depends on your specific history, which is exactly what the free consult and medical review are for. You’ll complete a detailed history so our clinical team can assess candidacy — usually within three business days — before anyone asks you to commit to anything.
